Each device is made of a container (normally cylinder, sometimes a box) which has legs attached on the sides and a mold on the bottom side that determines how the piece (or product as I call it) is formed. Cylinder is filled with clay then a piston is placed on top. I hit the piston with a hammer and with every impact force produced by it extrudes clay out of the cylinder in intervals - bigger the force longer the interval.

The beginning

March 2019, Ljubljana, Slovenia

Original idea was to create recordings of work made by the hammer while it extrudes clay out of the cylinder - for precise measuring it was also necessary to create a hammer that enabled measuring exact force of impact for each hit. Later more devices were introduced.
Each is made of a body (usually cylinder, sometimes a box) which has legs attached on the sides and a mold on the bottom that determines how the piece (i. e. product) is formed. Body is filled with clay then a piston is placed on top. I hit the piston with a hammer and with every impact clay is extruded out of the cylinder in intervals.
--> Original idea was abandoned due to problems listed below. I focused more on the creation of different devices (five in total) and exploration of possibilites to create different products: changing the diameter or even shape of container, changing the mould on the bottom etc.
Problems I did not forsee: -as more clay gets extruded the intervals, even tough the force delivered remains unchanged, become longer because of clay’s own weight; -position of the piston is not always vertical because it’s 'fixed' to the body only with its head; upper part is not touching anything so it starts to lean. Because of this it has to be constantly checked and moved into correct position. -device and hammer are both saperate units; device moves out of center of impact with each hit and also needs to be reppositioned frequently.

Second phase

October 2019, Belgrade, Serbia

Second phase is an upgrade of the first one as I had focused on the aestethics of the device and elimination of the problems which occured before.
There is only one device now instead of five - this was made possible by welding a laser-cut 'head' onto the bottomside of the cylinder (i.e. body); triangular head has a hole cut in the center with the same diameter as the body’s inside so clay can easily pass trough; around it there are three holes for the screws. To create different shapes laser-cut mounts are attached onto the head with the screws. Also problem with piston leaning in and whole device moving out of the zone of hammer’s impact is solved. For piston a guide is placed on top of the body, thus creating second fixed point (first is piston’s head) that keeps it always in vertical position; to keep the device in one place, new hammer was made which has legs that attatch to the legs of the device so they become connected.

This is a prototipe device which uses a blade set under an angle to scratch clay in thin layers. It consists of metal framework, a box for the clay that has no top side and has a moving bottom one - with the rotation of the screw bottom is lifted up or down. On the top of the framework are two rails on which a plate is placed. On te front of the plate a blade is monuted.

Clay is carefully placed into the box so there is no air pockets then it is raised untill the blade touches it - now it is set, plate is pushed away from the box, clay is raised to desired height and a scratch is made. Whit each scratch clay starts to build up forming different textures depending on how much clay was raised between each scratch. At certain height clay is no longer scratched but cut instead.

Festival theme was: 'What will happen after?'
Vinča is a village near Belgrade where city's depony is located. Depony is often set on fire.
'Vinča gori, mene boli'- if translated literaly it means: 'Vinča is burning, I am in pain', but 'boli' is acctualy a very common figure of speech expressing lack of interest - it can be read as "I really don't care'

'Vinča gori, mene boli'

27.10.2019:
Air pollution is alarmingly high: thermoplants, traffic, Vinča.
Almost no one wears a mask
Almost no one cares about what will happen after.
My lungs hurt, my head hurt.

Now I don't care.

WHAT WILL HAPPEN AFTER?
I will continue to sculpt.
In half a year I am leaving Belgrade.
