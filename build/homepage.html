Each device is made of a container (normally cylinder, sometimes a box) which has legs attached on the sides and a mold on the bottom side that determines how the piece (or product as I call it) is formed. Cylinder is filled with clay then a piston is placed on top. I hit the piston with a hammer and with every impact force produced by it extrudes clay out of the cylinder in intervals - bigger the force longer the interval.

The beginning

March 2019, Ljubljana, Slovenia Original idea was to create recordings of work made by the hammer while it extrudes clay out of the cylinder - for precise measuring it was necessary to create a hammer that enabled me to determine exact force off impact for each hit.
Problems I did not forsee: as more clay gets trough the intervals, even tough the force delivered remains unchanged, become longer because of clay’s own weight; alligment of the piston is not always vertical, because the hammer and the machine are two saperate pieces - hammer’s head does not always land with its centre on the piston (anomalities in the cylinder and in shape of pistons head also contribute to the movement of the piston)
--> Original idea was later abandoned due to probleems listed above. I focused more on the creation of different machines and exploration of possibilites to create different products: changing the diameter or even shape of container, changing the mould on the bottom etc.

Second phase

October 2019, Belgrade, Serbia How to get rid of the problems? New machine - just one machine with possibility of changing moulds. Laser cut! A guide to hold the piston in vertical position (interractive piece?)
new hammer - made to be screwed onto the machine so they become one whole. (eliminates problem of machine going away from the “impact field”). Posibillity of removeable legs - easier transport.
